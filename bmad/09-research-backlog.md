# Research Backlog

## Status

Draft. These research tasks should be completed before finalizing MVP scope, pricing, or guarantees.

## Research Goal

Ensure the SaaS is built based on real UMKM needs, proven sales/marketing practices, accurate unit economics, and safe compliance boundaries.

## 1. Customer Discovery Research

Questions:
- Which UMKM segments feel the strongest pain around lead generation, follow-up, quotation, and sales tracking?
- Which segments have enough margin to justify a subscription?
- Which segments already spend money on ads, agencies, or software?
- Which segments have repeat-order potential?
- Which segments can provide product photos/portfolio/testimonials?

Target interviews:
- Jersey/custom apparel owners
- Printing/packaging owners
- Custom manufacturing owners
- Catering/event owners
- Furniture/custom build owners
- B2B local suppliers

Interview topics:
- Monthly leads
- Current lead sources
- Monthly ad spend
- Closing rate
- Average order value
- Gross margin estimate
- Follow-up problems
- Quotation process
- Customer repeat behavior
- Current tools
- Willingness to pay

## 2. Sales & Marketing Playbook Research

Questions:
- Which sales tasks are proven to increase conversion for small businesses?
- What follow-up cadence works for consultative/local B2B/B2C sales?
- What makes outreach relevant instead of spammy?
- What daily/weekly sales routines do professional sales teams use?
- What should an AI agent recommend at each funnel stage?

Research outputs:
- Follow-up cadence playbook
- Lead qualification framework
- Prospect scoring framework
- Quotation follow-up playbook
- Repeat order playbook
- Campaign planning playbook
- Lost deal analysis framework

## 3. Prospect Discovery Source Research

Questions:
- Which prospect sources are safe, legal, and valuable?
- Which public data sources allow commercial use?
- Which sources require API/partnership?
- How to validate prospect relevance?
- How to avoid personal data abuse?

Possible sources to evaluate:
- Public business directories
- Event directories
- Tender/RFQ pages
- Business websites
- User-provided lists
- Existing customer data
- Referrals
- Local associations/communities
- Partner datasets

Output:
- Source whitelist
- Source risk rating
- Data fields allowed
- Collection method
- Rate limits
- Validation process

## 8. Pilot Design

Pilot goals:
- Validate value proposition.
- Validate daily task usefulness.
- Validate prospect quality.
- Validate follow-up and quotation impact.
- Validate cost-to-serve.
- Validate owner engagement.

Pilot structure:
- 5–10 merchants
- 1–3 verticals
- 30–90 days
- Weekly check-in
- Manual/concierge support
- Cost and result tracking

Pilot success indicators:
- Owner uses daily task board.
- Prospects are considered relevant.
- Follow-up completion improves.
- Quotations increase.
- Pipeline becomes visible.
- Some deals/orders are attributed to agent-assisted activity.
- Merchant says they would pay for continued use.
